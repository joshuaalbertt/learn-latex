\section{Writing Formulas}
Let's begin with a formula $e^{i\pi} + 1 = 0$, which is known as Euler's identity. It beautifully connects five fundamental mathematical constants: $e$, $i$, $\pi$, $1$, and $0$.

$$\int_{a}^{b} f(x) \, dx = F(b) - F(a)$$

\item We can do another:
$$\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^2} = \frac{\pi^2}{6}$$

\end{bmatrix}$$

\item We can also use continuous fractions:

\section{More Formulas}
\begin{itemize}

\item Integral:
$$\int_{a}^{b} f(x) \, dx = F(b) - F(a)$$

\item Triple integral:
$$\iiint_{a}^{b} \int_{c}^{d} f(x,y) \, dx \, dy$$

\item Vector:
$$\vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix} v_1 \\ v_2 \\ v_3 \end{bmatrix}$$

\item Limit:
$$\lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{1}{x} = 0$$
